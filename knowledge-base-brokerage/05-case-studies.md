# Case Studies

> **A note on these case studies:** Agentwerke is in our first year as a vertical-focused firm for broker-dealers. The examples below are illustrative — they show the kind of work we do and the results that are realistic. As we close named engagements (with permission), we'll replace these with verified results.

## Case study 1 (illustrative): Independent broker-dealer, 280 reps

**The situation**
A mid-Atlantic independent BD with 280 1099 reps across 32 OSJ branches. Annual rep onboarding pace: 35-45. The compliance team — one CCO, three analysts, two ops coordinators — was structurally underwater. The CCO was working 60-hour weeks; review queues were 5-7 days behind; the most recent FINRA branch audit cycle had taken 320 hours of senior staff time across three audited branches.

**What we built**
A Compliance Co-Pilot integrated with the firm's Smarsh archiving system, RegEd compliance management platform, Microsoft 365, and HubSpot CRM. Three agent functions:

1. **Email and social pre-screening** — every rep email and social post pulled from Smarsh, classified by risk tier, with first-pass approve/escalate recommendations and drafted analyst commentary
2. **OBA disclosure interview and drafting** — when a rep submits a new outside business activity, the agent runs a structured interview, drafts the disclosure form and conflict-of-interest analysis, and stages for principal approval
3. **Branch audit response package builder** — when FINRA gives the firm 30 days' notice on a branch audit, the agent compiles 24 months of records (supervisory reviews, training records, advertising approvals, OBAs, gift logs, blotters) into the response package

**The result**
Six months in: review queue down to under 24 hours; the CCO's hours back to 45-50/week; the most recent branch audit took 95 hours of senior staff time, down from ~110 average. With the existing compliance team's effective capacity meaningfully larger, the firm paused a planned compliance analyst hire.

**Engagement details**
Compliance Co-Pilot tier ($40,000 setup + $4,000/month). 12-week build. 90-day check-in showed the team plus the Co-Pilot moving ~22,000 review items per month at >97% successful disposition rate (correct human-final disposition agreed with the agent's recommendation).

## Case study 2 (illustrative): Growth-mode independent BD, 95 reps

**The situation**
A southeast regional BD that had been recruiting 4-6 reps per quarter from wirehouses. Their pitch to recruits centered on "we make the operational stuff easier" — but the actual onboarding experience was 30-45 days of paperwork hell. Half the recruits they signed letters of intent with dropped out before transitioning their book.

**What we built**
An Onboarding Accelerator integrated with the firm's CRM, document management system, and FINRA Web CRD. The agent:

- Walks new reps through document collection in a guided interview format (driver's license, FINRA forms, state registration prerequisites, OBAs)
- Drafts U4 amendments and supporting documentation from the rep interview, surfaces gaps for human review
- Manages the 21-day bridge license arrangement timing and notifications
- Pre-fills state registration applications across the rep's intended footprint
- Coordinates branch office paperwork with the assigned OSJ supervisor

**The result**
Average time from signed LOI to fully transitioned rep dropped from 38 days to 22 days. The rep dropout rate during the onboarding window dropped from ~50% to ~15%. The firm's recruiting pitch ("we'll have you trading new business in three weeks, not six") became materially true.

**Engagement details**
Onboarding Accelerator tier ($25,000 setup + $2,000/month). 8-week build. After six months: agent had handled 21 rep onboardings; payback achieved at month 4 measured against avoided ops-coordinator hire.

## What both engagements have in common

- The team, with the agent doing the heavy lifting, absorbed the **structured, high-volume 60-75% of the work** together. Judgment calls, escalations, and complex cases stayed with humans, where they belong.
- The agent integrated with the **existing tech stack** — no platform migration required.
- The build delivered in **8-12 weeks** from signed SOW.
- Every regulatory output passed through human approval. The agent extended supervisory capacity; it didn't replace supervision.
- Both clients reached **payback within five to seven months** measured against what equivalent capacity through hiring would have cost (or the audit-cycle time saved).
